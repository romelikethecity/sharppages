Law Firm Website Design: What Clients Look For

By Rome Thorndike

What Clients Evaluate

A potential client visiting your law firm's website is evaluating three things in 10 seconds: "Do they handle my type of case?", "Are they credible?", and "How do I contact them?"

Most law firm websites fail on the first question. The homepage says "Full-Service Law Firm" with a stock photo of a gavel. The visitor cannot immediately tell if you handle personal injury, family law, estate planning, or corporate litigation. They leave.

Winning law firm websites lead with practice areas prominently, display attorney credentials immediately, and put the phone number and contact form above the fold on every page.

Practice Area Pages

Each practice area needs its own page, not a bullet point on a general services page. "Personal Injury Attorney in [City]" is a separate page from "Car Accident Lawyer in [City]": different search queries, different intent, different content.

Each practice area page should include: what types of cases you handle in this area, your results (verdicts, settlements, outcomes), the process a client can expect, and a clear CTA to schedule a consultation.

Practice area pages are your primary SEO assets. They target "[practice area] lawyer [city]" searches, which are the highest-intent legal searches. These pages should be optimized with local keywords, schema markup (LegalService), and internal links to related practice areas.

Attorney Bio Pages

Clients hire attorneys, not firms. Each attorney needs a dedicated page with: professional photo, bar admissions, education, years of experience, practice focus areas, notable results, and professional memberships.

Attorney bio pages rank for "[Attorney Name]" searches. When a referral says "call John Smith," the prospect Googles the name. Your attorney page needs to be the top result with enough credibility to confirm the referral.

Bio pages are also E-E-A-T signals for Google. In legal content (YMYL), author expertise directly affects how Google evaluates your content's trustworthiness.

Trust Signals That Matter

Law firm visitors are often in stressful situations (injury, divorce, criminal charge, business dispute). Trust signals reduce anxiety and move visitors toward contact:

  • Case results with numbers. "$2.3M settlement in trucking accident case" is more persuasive than "significant verdicts and settlements." Include appropriate disclaimers ("past results do not guarantee future outcomes") as required by your state bar.
  • Awards and recognitions. Super Lawyers, Best Lawyers, Martindale-Hubbell ratings, state bar leadership positions. Display badges prominently on the homepage and attorney pages.
  • Client testimonials. Direct quotes from past clients about their experience. Video testimonials perform better than text. Place them on practice area pages near the contact CTA.
  • Professional photography. Stock photos of gavels and courthouses signal a generic, low-investment website. Professional photos of your actual attorneys, office, and team signal credibility and investment in the practice.
  • Response time commitment. "We respond to all inquiries within 2 hours" or "Free consultation within 24 hours" reduces the anxiety of reaching out. Make the commitment specific and keep it.

Local SEO for Law Firms

Legal searches are local. "Personal injury lawyer near me" and "divorce attorney [city]" are the highest-value keywords in legal marketing.

The local SEO checklist applies with these law-firm-specific additions:

  • Google Business Profile: Complete every field. Add attorney photos, office photos, and case result posts. Respond to every review. GBP is often a client's first interaction with your firm.
  • Legal directories: Avvo, FindLaw, Justia, Lawyers.com, and your state bar directory. Keep NAP (Name, Address, Phone) consistent across all listings. These directories also pass link authority to your site.
  • Practice area + city pages: "Car Accident Lawyer Dallas" and "Car Accident Lawyer Fort Worth" are separate pages with unique content. Use the hub-and-spoke model with a practice area hub linking to city-specific pages.

Law firm SEO is competitive and expensive in paid search ($50-200+ per click for injury keywords). Organic rankings through content and local SEO deliver leads at a fraction of PPC cost over time. Learn the fundamentals in Google's SEO starter guide.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do law firm websites need to include disclaimers?

Yes. Most state bar associations require attorney advertising disclaimers. Common requirements: 'This is attorney advertising,' 'Past results do not guarantee future outcomes,' and 'No attorney-client relationship is formed by visiting this website.' Check your state bar's advertising rules.

Should I show case results on my website?

Yes, with appropriate disclaimers. Verdicts and settlement amounts are powerful trust signals. Include 'Past results do not guarantee future outcomes' disclaimers as required by your state bar. Specific numbers are more persuasive than vague 'successful outcomes.'

How important is mobile for law firm websites?

Critical. Over 60% of legal searches happen on mobile. Many potential clients search while in a stressful situation (accident, arrest, injury) using their phone. If your site does not work on mobile, you lose those clients to competitors who invested in mobile design.
