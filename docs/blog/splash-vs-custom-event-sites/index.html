Splash vs Custom Event Sites: Feature and Cost Comparison

By Rome Thorndike

What Splash Offers

Splash is an enterprise event marketing platform. It provides:

  • Branded event pages with a visual editor
  • Registration and ticketing
  • Email marketing workflows tied to event actions
  • Event analytics (page views, registration conversion, attendance)
  • Guest list management with status tracking
  • Onsite check-in tools (QR code scanning)

It is a full-featured solution for marketing teams that run events frequently. The visual editor is well-designed. The analytics are useful. The email workflows save time on recurring events.

The cost: $1,000 to $5,000+ per month depending on features and event volume. Annual commitment typically required. That is $12,000 to $60,000 per year before you run a single event.

What Custom Event Pages Offer

A custom event page provides:

  • Fully branded design matching your website (no platform branding)
  • Registration form with custom fields
  • Payment processing via Stripe (2.9% + $0.30 per transaction)
  • GA4 and Meta Pixel tracking for attribution
  • 90+ mobile PageSpeed (vs. Splash's 50-70)
  • Mobile-optimized experience
  • Full data ownership (data in your CRM or spreadsheet)

What custom does not include: built-in email workflows (use your existing email tool like Mailchimp, HubSpot, or ActiveCampaign), a visual editor for self-service changes (updates go through a developer), and onsite check-in tools (use a spreadsheet or a third-party check-in app).

Cost: $2,000 to $4,000 one-time build. $0/month ongoing. Clones for recurring events: $500 to $1,000 each.

The savings are not marginal. Custom event pages cost 80-85% less than Splash over three years for most organizations. The only question is whether Splash's workflow automation saves enough staff time to justify the premium.

For teams with a dedicated event marketer who creates pages weekly, Splash's visual editor has value. For teams where events are managed by a marketing generalist who runs 1-2 events per month, paying a developer for quick updates is more cost-effective than a $36,000/year platform.

Performance and Conversion Rates

Splash pages score 50-70 on mobile PageSpeed. Custom event pages score 90-99. That 20-40 point gap has a direct impact on registration conversion rates.

A registration page that loads in 1.2 seconds (typical for custom static) converts at a higher rate than one that loads in 3.5 seconds (typical for Splash). For an event driving 5,000 visitors to the registration page, even a 0.5% conversion rate improvement adds 25 additional registrations.

At $200 per ticket, those 25 registrations are worth $5,000, which is enough to cover the entire cost of a custom event page build. The performance advantage pays for itself in additional conversions, on top of the platform fee savings.

Test your current event pages with Google's Core Web Vitals assessment. If your LCP is above 2.5 seconds or your Total Blocking Time exceeds 200ms, your registration page is losing potential attendees to slow load times.

When Each Makes Sense

Choose Splash if: You run 20+ events per year, your marketing team needs self-service page creation without developer involvement, you rely on email workflows tied to event registration triggers, and the $12,000-60,000 annual cost is justified by operational efficiency.

Choose custom if: You run 1-15 events per year, you want zero recurring platform costs, page load speed matters (Splash pages score 50-70 on PageSpeed vs. 90+ for custom), you want full data ownership, or your events follow a repeatable format that benefits from template cloning.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can custom pages match Splash's design quality?

Yes. Custom pages are designed for your brand with no template constraints. The design quality is limited only by the designer, not the platform. Custom pages also load 30-50% faster than Splash pages, which improves the visitor experience.

What about Splash's event analytics?

GA4 and Meta Pixel provide equivalent (often superior) analytics. You see traffic sources, registration conversion rates, and post-event engagement. The data lives in your GA4 account, not a third-party platform, and integrates with your other marketing data.
