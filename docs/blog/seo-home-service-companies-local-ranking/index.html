SEO for Home Service Companies: Ranking in Your Local Market

By Rome Thorndike

How Homeowners Search

Home service searches are urgent and local. "Emergency plumber near me" at 11pm. "AC repair [city]" in July. "Electrician [neighborhood]" when the power goes out. These searches have immediate purchase intent: the homeowner needs help now.

Appearing in the Google Map Pack for these searches is the primary goal. The Map Pack shows the top 3 local businesses with phone numbers, reviews, and directions. Most homeowners call one of the three Map Pack results without scrolling to organic results.

Your entire SEO strategy should prioritize Map Pack visibility for your core services in your service area.

The Local SEO Playbook

Follow the local SEO checklist with these home service-specific additions:

  • Service + city pages. Create a page for every service in every city you serve: "Plumbing Repair in [City A]," "Plumbing Repair in [City B]." Each page needs unique content about serving that area, not just a city name swap.
  • Emergency keywords. If you offer emergency services, optimize for "emergency [service] [city]" and "24 hour [service] near me." These high-intent keywords have high conversion rates.
  • Service area schema. Use LocalBusiness schema with your full service area defined. This tells Google exactly which geographic areas you serve.
  • Photo evidence. Google Business Profile photos of completed work (before/after), branded vehicles, uniformed technicians. Visual proof of real local service builds trust and engagement.

Reviews Win Local

In home services, reviews are the number one differentiator. A plumber with 150 reviews at 4.8 stars dominates a plumber with 12 reviews at 5.0 stars.

Build a review system: text or email every customer a direct Google review link within 24 hours of service completion. Make it one tap. Train technicians to mention reviews at the end of each job. Respond to every review (positive and negative) within 24 hours.

Negative reviews are not disasters. A professional, empathetic response to a negative review often builds more trust than five-star reviews. Potential customers read your response to see how you handle problems.

Service Area Pages That Rank

The most common mistake with service area pages: creating 20 pages with identical content and swapping the city name. Google penalizes this as thin, duplicate content. Each page needs unique substance.

What makes a service area page unique:

  • Local details. Mention neighborhoods, landmarks, or common housing types. "Most homes in [City] were built in the 1970s with copper plumbing that is now reaching end of life" is specific and useful.
  • Service-specific content. "Water heater installation in [City]" should discuss local water hardness, common water heater types in the area, and permit requirements for that city.
  • Response time for that area. "We reach [City] in 30 minutes from our [Location] office." This is useful information that differs by service area and signals local presence.
  • Local reviews. Feature reviews from customers in that city on the city page. This creates unique social proof content while reinforcing local relevance.

Use the hub-and-spoke model: a service hub page ("Plumbing Services") linking to city-specific pages. Each city page links back to the hub and cross-links to related services in the same city.

Technical SEO for Home Services

Home service websites have specific technical requirements that affect local rankings:

Page speed on mobile. Homeowners searching for emergency services are on their phones. If your site takes 4 seconds to load, they call the next result. Target 90+ on PageSpeed Insights mobile. Read our guide on reaching a 90+ score.

Click-to-call on every page. The phone number should be a tap-to-call link in the header of every page. For emergency services, make it the most prominent element on mobile. Format: <a href="tel:+15551234567">.

LocalBusiness schema. Include your business name, address, phone, service area, hours, and service types in structured data on every page. Use Google's guidelines for proper markup. This feeds the Knowledge Panel and can trigger rich results.

Service schema. Mark up each service page with Service schema including service type, area served, and price range. This helps Google understand exactly what you offer and where.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many service area pages do I need?

Create a page for each primary city or neighborhood you serve. If you serve 10 cities, create 10 location pages. Each page needs unique content, not just the city name changed. Google penalizes thin duplicate content.

Should I list prices on my website?

For standard services with fixed pricing, yes. 'Drain cleaning starting at $149' helps visitors self-qualify. For variable services (remodeling, custom work), show ranges or 'starting at' prices. Visitors who cannot find pricing information often leave.

How do I compete with HomeAdvisor and Angi in search?

You cannot outrank aggregators for broad terms like 'plumber near me.' But you can outrank them for specific terms like 'tankless water heater installation [city]' with dedicated service pages and local SEO. Focus on specific services in specific cities rather than broad categories.
