Squarespace Alternatives

Squarespace makes it easy to get a site online. But it scores 55-65 on mobile PageSpeed, charges $16-$49/month forever, and gives you zero code ownership. If your site is a business asset, not a hobby project, here are the alternatives worth considering.

Why People Leave Squarespace

Squarespace is the easiest website builder to use. The templates are attractive. The editor is intuitive. For someone who needs a basic web presence and has no interest in technical details, Squarespace does its job. The problems show up later.

PageSpeed scores that cap at 55-65 on mobile. Squarespace loads a substantial JavaScript framework, multiple stylesheets, and platform tracking code on every page. Even a simple five-page business site with optimized images will score in the 55-65 range on Google PageSpeed Insights for mobile. This is not something you can fix with image compression or fewer sections. It is baked into the platform.

Monthly fees that add up. Squarespace's Business plan costs $33/month ($396/year). The Basic Commerce plan is $36/month ($432/year). Over three years, a Business plan costs $1,188 in hosting alone. That is before any add-ons, email campaigns, or scheduling features Squarespace charges extra for.

No code ownership. When you build on Squarespace, Squarespace owns the infrastructure. You cannot export your site design. You cannot take your templates, your layouts, or your custom CSS and move them to another host. If you leave Squarespace, you start over. Your content exports as a basic XML file, but the design does not come with it.

Limited SEO control. Squarespace handles the basics: title tags, meta descriptions, alt text, and a sitemap. But you cannot add custom schema markup without code injection workarounds. You cannot create programmatic pages for local SEO. You cannot control your robots.txt in a meaningful way. You cannot implement advanced internal linking strategies without manually editing every page. For businesses competing on search, these limitations add up. Read our Squarespace vs. custom website comparison for the full picture.

Design constraints disguised as simplicity. Squarespace templates look good out of the box. But try to deviate from the template structure and you hit walls. Custom layouts require code injection. Unique interaction patterns are difficult or impossible. The simplicity that made Squarespace appealing at the start becomes a constraint as your business grows.

Static HTML

The fastest and cheapest option. Static HTML sites consistently score 90-99 on mobile PageSpeed because there is no framework overhead, no server-side processing, and no third-party scripts loading by default. Hosting on GitHub Pages or Cloudflare Pages costs nothing. You own every file and can move your site to any host at any time.

The tradeoff is that content updates require a developer or a build process. For business sites that change monthly or quarterly, this is not an issue. For blogs publishing daily, it creates friction.

WordPress

WordPress gives you the CMS that Squarespace has, plus full code ownership, unlimited SEO control, and a plugin ecosystem that covers nearly any feature you need. The downside is maintenance. WordPress sites require hosting ($10-$100/month), regular updates, security monitoring, and occasional troubleshooting when plugins conflict. A well-optimized WordPress site scores 65-80 on mobile PageSpeed.

Webflow

Webflow is a step up from Squarespace in terms of design flexibility and SEO control. The visual builder is more powerful. You get cleaner markup and better PageSpeed scores (typically 55-85 on mobile depending on complexity). But Webflow still charges monthly fees ($14-$39/site) and still locks you into the platform for design work. See our Webflow alternatives page for a deeper comparison.

Wix

Wix occupies similar territory to Squarespace: easy to use, template-based, no code ownership. Wix has improved its SEO tools and page speed over the years, but most Wix sites still score 50-65 on mobile PageSpeed. Moving from Squarespace to Wix trades one set of limitations for a similar set. If you are going to migrate, choose a platform that solves the problems you are leaving Squarespace over.

When Squarespace Is Fine

Not every site needs to leave Squarespace. The platform works well for specific use cases.

Portfolios and personal sites. If your site exists to showcase work and you do not depend on search traffic, Squarespace is fine. The templates are designed for visual presentation. Speed matters less when people arrive via direct links or social media rather than Google.

Small businesses that do not compete on search. A local restaurant, a hair salon with word-of-mouth referrals, a freelancer who gets clients through networking. If Google rankings are not part of your growth strategy, Squarespace's limitations on speed and SEO are less relevant.

People who want zero technical involvement. Squarespace handles hosting, security, SSL, backups, and software updates. You never think about any of it. For someone who wants to set up a site and forget the technical side entirely, that has real value.

When You Should Leave Squarespace

The case for leaving Squarespace becomes clear when your website is a primary growth channel.

You are competing on search. If you need to rank for competitive keywords, every PageSpeed point matters. A site scoring 60 on mobile is giving up ground to competitors scoring 90+. Google uses Core Web Vitals as a ranking signal, and Squarespace's performance ceiling makes it difficult to compete in search-heavy markets.

You need programmatic pages. Local businesses that want a page for every city they serve. SaaS companies that want landing pages for every integration or use case. E-commerce sites with hundreds of product categories. Squarespace cannot generate pages programmatically. Each page is created manually.

You need custom functionality. Squarespace's feature set is fixed. You get what the platform offers. If you need custom forms, calculators, interactive tools, API integrations, or any feature Squarespace does not support natively, you are limited to code injection workarounds that break with platform updates.

You want to own your site. A business website is an asset. On Squarespace, you are renting that asset month by month. If Squarespace raises prices, changes features, or shuts down, your site goes with it. With static HTML or WordPress, you own the files. You can move them anywhere.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I move my Squarespace site to another platform?

Squarespace offers a limited XML export of blog posts and basic pages. But your design, custom CSS, layout configurations, and most page content do not export cleanly. In practice, migrating from Squarespace means rebuilding your site on the new platform. The content transfers. The design does not.

Why is my Squarespace site so slow on mobile?

Squarespace loads a large JavaScript framework, multiple CSS files, and tracking scripts on every page. This happens regardless of how simple your page is. The platform also serves images at sizes larger than necessary on mobile devices. These are platform-level decisions you cannot override.
