BTL Event Sites

Multi-city physician event registration pages. 8 specialties per city, 48-hour cloning, full attribution from ad click to completed registration.

The Challenge

One Event Format, Dozens of Cities

A medical device manufacturer needed registration sites for physician education events across multiple US cities. Each city required its own site with GA4 tracking, Meta Pixel attribution, and registration forms.

The events covered 8 medical specialties: dermatology, psychiatry, orthopedics, plastic surgery, med spa, chiropractic, cosmetic dentistry, and family practice. Each specialty needed a dedicated landing page with targeted messaging and value propositions specific to that audience.

The traditional approach would mean building each city from scratch. Weeks of development per city, separate tracking setups, and costs that multiply linearly. The client needed an approach that could scale to 50+ cities without the budget scaling with it.

What We Built

Config-Driven Event Sites That Clone in Hours

27 Pages Per City
8 Specialty Pages
48hrs Clone Time
$0/mo Hosting

Each city runs from a single config file. Venue, dates, capacity, local details. Change the config, rebuild the site. The first city took 5 to 7 days to design, build, and launch. Every additional city clones in 48 hours with fresh venue details, local references, and separate tracking.

Each of the 8 specialty pages has custom messaging, local testimonials, and targeted value propositions for that physician audience. The full-day event format includes breakfast demos, a keynote session, specialty breakouts, 1-on-1 consultations, and a networking dinner.

All pages score 90+ on Google PageSpeed mobile. No heavy frameworks, no CMS overhead. Static HTML deployed to free hosting.

Engaged Visitor Tracking

We only counted visitors who stayed 10+ seconds on the page, filtering out accidental clicks and bot traffic. At $0.30 per engaged visitor, every dollar went toward people who actually read the event details.

Each city gets its own tracking pixel. No cross-event contamination. Performance data stays clean, and you can compare results across cities with confidence in the numbers.

Retargeting kicked in at day 7. Anyone who visited the registration page but didn't sign up saw follow-up ads with the full event agenda and countdown messaging. The campaign progressed from awareness to urgency, with copy updates matching the event timeline.

The first city is where the design, page structure, and tracking architecture get built. Every city after that inherits all of it. You get the same 27-page site, the same 8 specialty landing pages, the same tracking setup. The only things that change are the venue, dates, and local details.

Currently live in Minnesota and Ohio, with infrastructure ready for nationwide expansion. The template carries over the proven design, conversion flow, and page structure. Adding a new city is a config change, not a rebuild.

FAQ

Common Questions

How long does it take to launch a new city?

The first city takes 5 to 7 business days. Each additional city clones in 48 hours. We swap the venue, dates, local details, and tracking codes. The design, page structure, and specialty content carry over.

Can each city have its own ad campaign and tracking?

Yes. Every city gets its own Facebook Pixel, GA4 property, and ad campaigns. No cross-event data contamination. You can compare performance across cities with clean attribution.

What happens to the registration sites after the event?

You own all the files. The sites stay live as long as you want them. Many clients keep them up as evergreen brand pages or redirect them to the next event. Hosting is free on GitHub Pages or Cloudflare Pages.
