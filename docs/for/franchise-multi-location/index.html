Web Design & SEO for Franchise & Multi-Location

Franchises need consistent branding across every market with local SEO for each location. Most franchise websites are a single corporate site (no local SEO) or a collection of inconsistent location pages (no brand consistency). The template cloning model solves both.

The Problem

Your corporate site has one 'locations' page listing 25 addresses. None of those locations rank individually for local search.

Each franchisee built their own site. Designs are inconsistent, branding drifts, and nobody knows which sites have tracking.

Your agency charges per location for SEO. At $1,500/mo across 25 markets, the annual spend is $450,000. Rankings do not justify the cost.

Why Franchise & Multi-Location Need a Different Approach

Franchise SEO fails two ways. A single corporate site with one locations page: Google treats it as one site, individual locations do not rank. Or 25 independently built sites: inconsistent quality, fragmented brand. The template model splits the difference.

One master template enforces brand consistency. Each location gets unique local content: team bios, service area descriptions, local reviews, address-specific schema. Google sees 25 locally relevant pages, not one generic corporate site.

Programmatic generation makes multi-location SEO economical. Instead of $1,500/mo per location, the build script generates all location pages from structured data. 25 locations with 10 service pages = 250 pages in a single build.

Tracking per location matters for franchise performance measurement. Each location gets its own analytics. Corporate sees aggregate traffic. Franchisees see their own performance. Ad campaigns target each market separately.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can each location have unique content?

Yes. The template provides consistent structure. Each location gets unique team bios, service area content, reviews, and local details.

How do you handle 25+ locations?

Programmatic generation. Build the template once, generate location pages from a data file. Adding a location = adding a row and rebuilding.

Can franchisees make local changes?

The data file approach lets franchisees update local details through a simple interface. Design is controlled centrally.

What about brand compliance?

The master template enforces brand guidelines. Individual locations customize content within the approved structure.
